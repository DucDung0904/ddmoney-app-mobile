# Design System Specification: High-End Light Mode

## 1. Overview & Creative North Star: "The Luminous Architect"
The Creative North Star for this design system is **"The Luminous Architect."** This philosophy moves away from the rigid, boxed-in nature of traditional fintech interfaces toward an editorial, airy experience that feels drafted by hand rather than generated by a template. 

To break the "standard" look, this system utilizes **Intentional Asymmetry** and **Tonal Depth**. Instead of centering every element, use generous, uneven white space to guide the eye. Overlap elements—such as a floating card partially breaking the boundary of a section—to create a sense of physical space. The goal is a professional interface that breathes, prioritizing clarity and premium "white space" over information density.

---

## 2. Colors & Surface Philosophy — "Ocean Blueprint"

The palette is anchored by **Ocean Blue (#185FA5)** as the singular brand identity, with four distinct semantic hues that are perceptually separated so they never compete.

### Color Strategy: Full Palette
Each hue plays an exclusive role. No hue is reassigned or reused across roles.

### Semantic Role Rules
- **Ocean Blue (#185FA5):** Header, CTA buttons, balance highlight, active nav, primary brand.
- **Income Green (#639922):** Income amounts, positive transactions — always "positive."
- **Expense Red (#E24B4A):** Expense amounts, alerts, warnings — natural "caution" signal.
- **Savings Teal (#1D9E75):** Savings labels and progress — calm, wealth-building feel.
- **Investment Amber (#EF9F27):** Investment highlights — stands out without conflicting.
- **Neutral Gray (#888780):** Secondary text, dividers, neutral icons — never semantic.

### Chip / Badge Composition Rule
```
bg    = *50   (very light, readable on white)
text  = *800  (high contrast)
border = *600 (anchor — same as button color)
```

### Button & Border Emphasis
Use **stop 600** for interactive borders and primary buttons. Gradient CTAs: `OceanBlue600` → `OceanBlue400` at 135°.

### The "No-Line" Rule
Traditional 1px solid borders for sectioning are strictly prohibited. Structural boundaries must be defined solely through background color shifts or tonal transitions.
*   **Example:** A `surface-container-low` (#F1F3F9) sidebar against a `background` (#F7F9FF) main area.

### Surface Hierarchy & Nesting
*   **Background (#F7F9FF):** The foundational canvas.
*   **Surface Container Lowest (#FFFFFF):** Primary content cards.
*   **Surface Container High (#E6E8EE):** Utility bars or secondary zones.

### The "Glass & Gradient" Rule
Glassmorphism for floating nav overlays: semi-transparent surface + `20px` backdrop-blur.
Signature CTA gradient: `OceanBlue600` (#185FA5) → `OceanBlue400` (#378ADD) at 135°.

## 3. Typography: Editorial Authority
We use **Manrope** not just for legibility, but as a brand anchor. The hierarchy is designed to feel like a high-end financial journal.

*   **Display (lg/md/sm):** Use for "Big Picture" numbers and welcoming headers. These should have a slight negative letter-spacing (-0.02em) to feel tighter and more premium.
*   **Headline & Title:** These are your "wayfinders." Keep them bold and clear. Pair a `headline-lg` (2rem) with a `body-md` (0.875rem) to create high-contrast layouts that feel sophisticated.
*   **Label (md/sm):** Reserved for micro-data (e.g., "Balance," "Transaction ID"). Use the `on_surface_variant` (#434656) to keep these present but secondary.

---

## 4. Elevation & Depth: Tonal Layering
Avoid the "stuck-on" look of traditional shadows. Depth must be earned through the **Layering Principle**.

*   **Ambient Shadows:** When a card must float, use an "Ambient Shadow." 
    *   **Blur:** 32px – 64px.
    *   **Opacity:** 4% – 6%.
    *   **Color:** Use a tinted version of `on_surface` (e.g., `rgba(24, 28, 32, 0.05)`).
*   **The "Ghost Border" Fallback:** If accessibility requires a stroke (e.g., on a white card against a white background), use a "Ghost Border": `outline_variant` (#C3C5D9) at **15% opacity**. Never use a 100% opaque border.
*   **Refraction:** For Glassmorphic layers, add a top-left 1px inner-border in `#FFFFFF` at 30% opacity to simulate light catching the edge of the "glass."

## 5. Components: The Refined Primitives

### Buttons
*   **Primary:** Gradient-filled (`primary` to `primary_container`), `xl` roundedness (1.5rem), and no border.
*   **Tertiary:** Text-only in `primary` (#003CC7) with no background, used for low-emphasis actions like "Cancel" or "View All."

### Cards & Lists
*   **Card Aesthetic:** Use `surface_container_lowest` (#FFFFFF) with a `lg` (1rem) corner radius. 
*   **No Dividers:** Lists should separate items using **8px to 16px of vertical white space** or a subtle hover state shift to `surface_container_low` (#F1F3F9).

### Input Fields
*   **State:** Default inputs use `surface_container_low` with no border. On focus, the background remains, but a 2px "Ghost Border" of `primary` appears at 40% opacity.
*   **Typography:** Labels use `label-md` placed *above* the field, never as a placeholder, to maintain accessibility.

### Floating Navigation (Signature Component)
Inspired by the provided reference, the navigation should be a floating "pill" using Glassmorphism.
*   **Background:** `surface` at 80% opacity with `backdrop-filter: blur(12px)`.
*   **Active State:** Use a high-contrast pill in `primary_container` (#0D51FB) to highlight the active route.

---

## 6. Do’s and Don’ts

### Do
*   **Do** use asymmetrical margins (e.g., a 64px left margin and a 120px right margin) to create a custom, editorial feel.
*   **Do** use the `tertiary` (#922400) color sparingly for high-attention alerts or unique financial highlights.
*   **Do** prioritize "Breathing Room." If a screen feels crowded, increase the white space between sections by 2x.

### Don't
*   **Don't** use black (#000000). Use `on_surface` (#181c20) for all "black" text to maintain the soft, premium light-mode aesthetic.
*   **Don't** use sharp corners. Every interactive element must use at least the `DEFAULT` (0.5rem) roundedness to feel approachable and modern.
*   **Don't** use shadows to solve hierarchy problems that can be solved with background color shifts.
